AI Governance Desk

Score preferences — then compare governance proposals

Conjoint preference scoring across safety versus innovation, public versus private, and international versus national — against naive tech-first baselines.

Open desk Read honesty

Problem

Tech-first proposals push innovation, private control, and national scope even when public preference leans the other way. Teams need a desk that scores those leans by domain and risk perception.

Product

AI Governance Desk runs conjoint preference scoring beside always- innovation, always-private, and always-national baselines, with jobs, audit, and goldens. A method experiment — not a regulator product.

Selling points

  • Compare preference-aligned proposals against always-innovation, always-private, and always-national baselines
  • Keep governance preference jobs honest with lifecycle, batch siblings, and audit export
  • Browse dual-impl goldens so preference scores stay checkable
  • Org webhooks and roles so rejects and rotates stay visible

Features

  • Org tenancy
  • Member roles
  • Project / governance domain catalog
  • Governance preference job CRUD
  • Lifecycle transitions
  • Illegal transition reject
  • Optimistic version conflict
  • Batch sibling transitions
  • Scenario compare (tech-first vs preference-aligned)
  • Audit log + CSV export
  • Goldens browser
  • Honesty page
  • Signed inbound webhooks
  • Admin webhook rotate
  • Pagination + search
  • Rate-limit feedback
  • Offline try page
  • Marketing landing
  • Preference clarity strip
  • Scenario JSON export

How it works

  1. Open the desk and create a governance preference job under a governance domain
  2. Run scenario compare — tech-first vs preference-aligned governance
  3. Advance lifecycle or batch, then export the audit trail

Honesty / limits

Workflow experiment inspired by the paper. Not a replacement for the authors' system. Not a government AI regulation product. Never brand as a government AI regulator or EU AI Act product.
