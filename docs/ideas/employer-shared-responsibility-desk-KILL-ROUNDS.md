# Employer Shared Responsibility Desk — G3 kill rounds

**Slug:** `employer-shared-responsibility-desk`  
**State:** `adversarial` — method-stress framing, **not** a GTM claim  
**Not** `ready_to_build`. No product folder.

Framing: these kills test whether the **lab method** (unique invariants + falsifiers) survives — not whether the desk “wins market share.”

## Kill A — Commercial ACA / IRS already wins

**Attempt:** IRS Letter 226-J / CP 220J and commercial ACA reporting platforms (benefits admin + 1094-C/1095-C filing vendors) already compute and assess employer shared-responsibility payments. Controllers do not need another toy.

**Answer (honesty, not idea death):** Correct commercially. Incumbents and the IRS own **production** assessments and filing. The lab claim is a **method / FP&A forecast** of the statutory month walk — (a)/(b) branch, offer gate, indexed monthly applicable amounts, (b)(2) cap — that spreadsheets routinely get wrong **before** the letter arrives. Digests must say: forecast of statutory assessable-payment **shape** only; IRS notice + counsel / vendor own the real number; reporting-penalty lines are out of scope.

**Concrete fence:** UI / digests: “Not a substitute for Letter 226-J, Forms 14764/14765, or your ACA filing vendor.”

**Outcome:** **Survive** with Kill A honesty fence (same pattern as Filing Penalty Desk / ptax4975 / depositgap). Method claim only.

## Kill B — Niche / infrequent

**Attempt:** Only ALEs care; many never see a Letter 226-J; a dedicated desk will not sustain.

**Answer:** ALE status (≥50 FTE + FTEs) is a large employer class, not a rare statute hobby. Frequency is **monthly counting** all year plus **episode** letters — denser than one-off customs liquidation toys, thinner than daily ops boards. The lab optimizes for **bulletproof unique math**, not SaaS TAM. If after any future smoke users still forecast with a single “headcount × annual penalty” cell and ignore the offer gate / (b)(2) cap → Kill B becomes a **product falsifier** (abandon).

**Outcome:** **Survive** for research; falsifier noted for a future G4 doc.

## Kill C — Offline / legal holds the value

**Attempt:** The hard part is disputing Form 14765 lines, proving affordability safe harbors, controlled-group allocation of the 30-employee reduction, appeals, and payment timing — not arithmetic. Software will not hold the value.

**Answer:** Partially true. That is why v0 **fences**:

1. **No** Letter 226-J response drafting / Form 14764 advocacy  
2. **No** affordability safe-harbor scoring as a black-box “pass” (inputs may be flags only; counsel owns the harbor)  
3. **No** controlled-group 30-employee allocation engine beyond an explicit toy parameter  
4. **No** Form 1094-C / 1095-C late-filing penalty conflation  
5. If domain experts reject the month-walk stack as useless vs “just wait for 226-J” in ≥2 real scenarios → abandon (future G4)

Software holds value only as an **honest (a)/(b) + gate + cap + index** calculator that prevents inventing cash.

**Outcome:** **Survive** with dispute / harbor / controlled-group / reporting-penalty fences; do **not** claim relief or notice engine.
