## One-line itch

Taxpayers and controllers budget a flat “late fee,” but **26 U.S.C. § 6651** stacks **failure-to-file** (5%/month, cap 25%) and **failure-to-pay** (0.5%/month, cap 25%) on a **net amount due** that shrinks with payments — and when both apply in the same month, **§ 6651(c)(1)** reduces the file penalty by the pay penalty for that month. After notice/demand, **§ 6651(d)** can bump the pay rate from 0.5% to **1%**/month. A >60-day late file also hits an indexed **minimum** file penalty (lesser of floor or 100% of underpayment).

## Problem

Wrong models either (a) add 5% + 0.5% without the (c)(1) offset, (b) apply percentages to gross tax instead of § 6651(b) net amount due, (c) ignore mid-stream payments that cut the pay base month-by-month, or (d) miss the (d) rate bump / minimum floor. Those errors invent or hide **literal IRS dollar additions**.

## Who

Controllers / tax leads at small–mid filers (and their CPA shops) who forecast cash when a return will be late or a balance will remain unpaid across months — not “everyone with a tax software login.”

## Frequency

Per late return / unpaid balance timeline (monthly fractions). Not daily ops; cash stakes compound across months until caps bind.

## Current painful workaround

Spreadsheet “5% × months” cells, IRS notice reverse-engineering, or CPA verbal ranges — often without encoding (c)(1), (b) netting, or (d) bump as executable invariants.

## Unique claim (G2 draft)

Forecast **combined § 6651 additions** as:

1. **FTF** months × 5% of § 6651(b)(1) net amount due, aggregate ≤ 25%, with indexed **minimum** when >60 days late (lesser of floor or 100% underpayment).  
2. **FTP** months × 0.5% (or **1%** after § 6651(d) day) of § 6651(b)(2)/(b)(3) unpaid-at-month-start base, aggregate ≤ 25%.  
3. For months where **both** (a)(1) and (a)(2) apply: **reduce FTF by that month’s FTP** per § 6651(c)(1), without dropping FTF below the statutory minimum when the minimum sentence applies.  
4. Reject flat “5% + 0.5% forever,” dual-approver costumes, and interest-as-penalty conflation (§ 6601 interest is out of scope for v0).

**If we remove monthly dual-addition stacking + (c)(1) same-month reduction + (b) net-amount bases + optional (d) bump / minimum floor, the remaining product is a generic late-fee % calculator isomorphic to neither ptax4975 (15%/yr then 100% excise) nor oshamult (serial OSHA GBP cuts) nor c1592 (customs culpability caps).**
