# lanehold autopsy (G2 kill)

## Claim as framed

> If we remove lane capacity + hold expiry, the remaining product is a generic reservation list.

## Why that fails IDEA_DEPTH G2

G2 fails when the “unique” rule is only:

- status rename
- **numeric ceiling/floor/days + dual signer**
- tenancy + RBAC + audit

Lane capacity is a **numeric ceiling**. Hold expiry is a **days/timer gate**. That is the dual-gate core with warehouse nouns.

## Prior lab evidence (same shape)

| Prior product | Shared shape |
|---------------|--------------|
| `loadbay` | Dock capacity ceiling (`weightKg ≤ maxWeightKg`) + dual control before depart |
| Dual-gate wave (`taxhold` cluster) | Ceiling/floor/days + dual signer |
| Archive noun-swaps (`yardshare`, `dockslip`, `skihold`, …) | Capacity / slip / hold reservation FSMs |

Removing “lane” and “hold” language leaves the same product as a dock-capacity reservation board.

## Honest mistake

Framed because it was “distinct from billing/math products” (settlecut / bondstrip / tariffstep). That is the wrong comparison set. G2 asks: distinct from **any** prior sustained product, including the shallow wave. Domain-shopping after math products without checking the capacity archive is how shallow ideas re-enter.

## Lesson for next seeds

1. Compare against the **archive dual-gate + capacity** cluster first, not only the last three method-stress IDs.
2. “Hold expiry” and “capacity” are instant-kill vocabulary unless tied to an external table with non-ceiling invariants.
3. Kill early on paper; do not spend a fixture corpus on a rename.
