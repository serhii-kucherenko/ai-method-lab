## Who

EHS / safety-compliance **analysts** and finance partners at multi-site employers who today forecast OSHA **proposed** civil penalties as “statutory max minus a flat discount stack” — then get surprised when **serial remaining-balance** math and **classification gates** (willful / repeat / FTA / high-gravity Quick Fix) cut the proposed amount differently than a spreadsheet “sum the %” cell.

Secondary: **auditors** who need an append-only trail of GBP inputs, reduction steps, and gate rejects — not a dual-approver penalty board.

Not the audience: counsel litigating citations, teams seeking an OIS / OSHA Information System replacement, or anyone who only needs a one-field “discount calculator.”

---

## Why

29 U.S.C. § 666 and 29 CFR § 1903.15 authorize proposed civil penalties. OSHA FOM Ch. 6 starts from a **gravity-based penalty (GBP)** and applies size / history / good faith / Quick Fix **serially on the remaining balance** — not from statutory maximum, and not as an additive percentage sum.

Classification gates matter: size is ineligible on willful/repeat; good faith and Quick Fix are blocked for willful / repeat / FTA (and Quick Fix for high-gravity serious). Finance that models “start at max, subtract 70%+30%+15%” invents a proposed number that never matches serial FOM-shaped math.

Kill A stands: consultants, settlement counsel, and existing calculators already model GBP + reductions. Vision survives only as a **serial FOM math / classification-gate forecast honesty** experiment — not as “we issue the citation.”
