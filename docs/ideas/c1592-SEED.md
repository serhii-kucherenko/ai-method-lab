# c1592 — research seed

**State:** `framed` (activated 2026-07-22 after ptax4975 sustained)  
**Id:** `c1592`

## One-line itch

Importers and trade counsel budget a single “penalty hit,” but **19 U.S.C. § 1592(c)** sets **different statutory maximums** by culpability (negligence / gross negligence / fraud) and by whether the violation caused a **loss of duties, taxes, and fees** — including a **lesser-of** domestic-value cap on the duty-loss multiples, and a **percent-of-dutiable-value** path when there is no duty loss.

## Primary statute

**19 U.S.C. § 1592(c)** maximum civil penalties; prior-disclosure caps in § 1592(c)(4) (separate honesty fence — not automatic investigation-start detection).

## Why $ stakes

Civil penalties are **literal CBP dollar liabilities**, often alongside back duties. Wrong branch (duty-loss vs no-loss) or wrong culpability multiple invents or hides large cash.

## Unique claim (G2 draft)

Forecast **statutory maximum** as:

| Culpability | Duty loss | Cap |
|-------------|-----------|-----|
| Fraud | any | domestic value |
| Gross negligence | yes | lesser of domestic value **or** **4×** loss of duties/taxes/fees |
| Gross negligence | no | **40%** of dutiable value |
| Negligence | yes | lesser of domestic value **or** **2×** loss |
| Negligence | no | **20%** of dutiable value |

Reject flat “2× duties always,” dual-approver costumes, and understating domestic value when it binds the lesser-of.

**If we remove culpability + duty-loss branching + lesser-of domestic value, the remaining product is a generic fee calculator isomorphic to neither oshamult (serial OSHA cuts) nor lesserof (drawback refund cap).**
