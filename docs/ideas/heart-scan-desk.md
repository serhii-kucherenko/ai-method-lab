## Paper in plain words

Cardiac CT scans need both detailed structure outlines and usable clinical phenotypes. Doing those in isolation, or only at one hospital, often fails when tools move to new sites. The paper describes a unified framework for comprehensive cardiac CT segmentation and phenotyping with human-in-the-loop annotation, vision foundation model development, multicenter evaluation, and clinical validation. No public authors’ code shipped with the paper.

## Unique claim (dual-gate — not isomorphic)

**Unified segmentation + phenotyping plans** (joint structure+phenotype pathway, human-in-loop review, multicenter-aware validation) vs **naive baselines** (segmentation-only, phenotype-from-raw-pixels-only, single-center unchecked). Soft desk simulation — not the authors’ model as a brand. Distinct from Heart Rhythm Desk (ECG contrastive learning).

## Build claim

Org desk for cardiac CT pathway jobs: projects, lifecycle, scenario compare (unified vs naive), audit, goldens, honesty, marketing landing — Next.js + shadcn.

## Honesty

Workflow experiment inspired by the paper — not a medical imaging product for diagnosis and not a claim to replace the authors’ foundation model. Never brand this desk as a clinical device.
